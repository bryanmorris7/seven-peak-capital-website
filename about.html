Our Origin

From Main Street Bethlehem to Institutional Markets

Seven Peak Capital didn't start with a fund. It started with a foreclosure in Bethlehem, Pennsylvania in 2016 — a single-family home acquired below market, renovated, refinanced, and held. That first deal was the beginning of a disciplined approach to real estate investing that has guided every subsequent decision.

From that single property, the portfolio grew — a 3-unit multifamily on Center Street, LP positions in institutional multifamily syndications across Texas, and eventually the formation of Seven Peak Capital as a formal investment manager dedicated to deploying private capital at scale.

Today, we manage a portfolio spanning direct ownership and LP co-investments across 790+ units in high-growth Sun Belt markets — and Income Fund I, our institutional private credit vehicle open to accredited investors.

2016
First Acquisition — Bethlehem, PA

Purchased a single-family foreclosure through the BRRRR strategy: bought, renovated, rented, refinanced, and held. The model that would define our investment approach.

2017
Portfolio Expansion — Multifamily

Acquired a 3-unit multifamily on Center Street, steps from Main Street Bethlehem. Executed a successful cash-out refinance and held long term.

2023–2024
LP Syndications — Texas Markets

Expanded into LP positions in institutional multifamily syndications, including Grand Riviera (206 units, Dallas-Fort Worth) through Lone Star Capital.

2025
Income Fund I — Now Open

Launched Seven Peak Income Fund I, a 506(c) private credit vehicle structured for accredited investors seeking consistent quarterly income backed by real estate.

Why Real Estate

Four Reasons We Invest in Real Assets

01
Passive Income

Real estate generates consistent cash flow through rental income and lending returns. Unlike public equities, income is distributed regularly regardless of short-term market volatility — providing investors with predictable, recurring distributions.

02
Tax Advantages

Real estate investments benefit from depreciation deductions, pass-through tax treatment, and favorable long-term capital gains rates. These structural advantages preserve a materially higher portion of total return compared to most other asset classes.

03
Demand Fundamentals

Demand for quality rental housing continues to significantly outpace supply in high-growth U.S. markets. Demographic tailwinds, constrained new construction, and continued migration to Sun Belt metros create durable structural support for multifamily values and rents.

04
Low Volatility

Private real estate and credit have historically exhibited low correlation to public equity and bond markets. This diversification characteristic helps preserve portfolio value during periods of broad market stress, making real assets a stabilizing allocation for most portfolios.
