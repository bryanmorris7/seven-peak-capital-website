Investment Thesis

Why Private Credit. Why Now.

Private credit has become one of the most compelling risk-adjusted opportunities in the current environment. As traditional bank lending has tightened, the gap between capital demand and institutional supply has created a structural lending premium — which we capture through disciplined origination.

Income Fund I deploys capital into short-duration real estate-secured instruments where principal risk is mitigated by conservative loan-to-value ratios. This structure allows us to generate consistent, contractual income rather than relying on asset appreciation or favorable exit conditions.

The result: predictable quarterly distributions, capital protected by real estate collateral, and returns that are largely uncorrelated to public market volatility.

Investor FAQ

Common Questions

Income Fund I is open exclusively to accredited investors as defined under Rule 501 of Regulation D. An accredited investor generally includes individuals with annual income exceeding $200,000 (or $300,000 combined with a spouse) in each of the two most recent years, or individuals with a net worth exceeding $1 million excluding primary residence. Accreditation is verified through Parallel Markets prior to investment.
The minimum investment in Income Fund I is $50,000. Distributions are paid quarterly to your designated bank account via ACH or wire transfer. The preferred return target is 10–12% per annum, paid in equal quarterly installments. Distribution timing and amounts are subject to fund performance and the terms outlined in the offering memorandum.
Income Fund I is structured as a Wyoming Limited Liability Company and is offered under Rule 506(c) of Regulation D, promulgated by the SEC. Under 506(c), the fund may publicly advertise its offering but must verify that all investors are accredited. Seven Peak Capital LLC serves as the manager of the fund. Full structural details, rights, and obligations are set forth in the Operating Agreement and Private Placement Memorandum.
Investor capital is deployed into private credit instruments that are primarily secured by real estate collateral. We underwrite all positions with conservative loan-to-value ratios, typically at or below 75% of the property value. This collateral cushion is designed to protect investor principal in the event of borrower default. All underwriting, documentation, and risk management practices are described in detail in the offering memorandum.
Income Fund I is not a liquid investment. Investor capital is committed for the duration of the fund's investment period. Redemptions are subject to the terms of the Operating Agreement and may be limited by available liquidity. Prospective investors should be prepared to treat this as an illiquid, long-term allocation. We strongly encourage all investors to review the full liquidity provisions in the offering memorandum before investing.
